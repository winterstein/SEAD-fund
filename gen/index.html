SEAD Fund

It is with great pleasure that the Scottish Education & Action for Development Trustee Board can announce that 2016 shall see the launch of a new initiative focused on the development and education of local communities across Scotland.

The newly announced ‘SEAD Fund’ will invite applications specifically from small community groups to apply for funds of up to £250 to run or kick start campaign activity that engages with people and has a demonstrable impact and links local issues with those being experienced by others around the world.

More details will be announced soon. The Trustee Board will be particularly interested in applications that focus campaign activity on the following areas; Women’s Rights, Young People and their Global rights, Climate Justice, Inequality, Poverty and Health Justice.

What We Fund

SEAD Fund grants are small grants for individuals or groups. Grants are typically £100 - £250. We fund positive action, and campaigning. We look for proposals where the grant will have the most impact -- for example, helping a new campaign to get started, or funding a specific concrete action for a local community. We do not normally fund travel, e.g. to visit a conference.

How to Apply

Send an email to seadfund (at) gmail.com telling us:

  1. A one sentence summary of the project.
  2. Your contact details, and if you're applying for a group, the contact details for your group. Please include: name, email, phone, address, and if you have one, then also your website and social media (e.g. Twitter) profile.
  3. How you will spend the money, and the total you require.
  4. If your project involves other funding too, then tell us the details of that.
  5. How you will link-in with community groups as a result of / follow-on from any grant.
  6. The impact you hope this project will have.

Values

SEAD has two main aims:

  • To challenge the causes of poverty, social injustice and environmental degradation.
  • To support the community-based movement for positive social change – people collectively tackling challenges which have both a local and global perspective.
Our work is based on the following principles:
  • People-powered solutions
  • A rootedness in mutual solidarity
  • We can all learn from each other
